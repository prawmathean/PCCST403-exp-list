Algorithm for Experiment 16: Disk Scheduling Algorithms

1. Start the program.
2. Read the initial head position and a list of disk requests.
3. SSTF (Shortest Seek Time First):
   a. Start from the current head position.
   b. Find the request in the list that is closest to the head.
   c. Move the head to that position and calculate the movement (seek time).
   d. Remove that request from the list and repeat until all requests are serviced.
4. LOOK Algorithm:
   a. Sort the requests in ascending order.
   b. Determine the direction of movement (e.g., increasing).
   c. Service all requests in that direction until the last request is reached.
   d. Reverse direction and service all remaining requests until the other end is reached.
   e. Calculate the total movement by summing the differences between consecutive head positions.
5. Print the servicing sequence and the total head movement for each algorithm.
6. Stop the program.
